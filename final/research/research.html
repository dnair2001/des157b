Source 2 - Opportunity Gap

This video gives a clear definition of "opportunity gap" through the use of real life examples. The opportunity gap is based on the availability of resources. The speaker then compares this to an achievement gap which is uneven distribution of educational results or benefits. We can close the achievement gap by confronting the opportunity gap. Achievement is directly related to opportunity. Also, a positive and encouraging school environment staffed with people who believe in their students can change lives. This video is from the Washington State Board of Education which is big organization showing its credibility because they are directly related to a school organization.

Source 3 - Solving the Achievement Gap through Equity, Not Equality

The achievement gap occurs when one student scores higher than another when they are grouped by gender, race, ethnicity, or socio-economic status. Equity would close this achievement gap by giving the needed support and support structures to those very different groups. Equality and Equity are actually two very different things. To close the achievement gap, we need to design a curriculum for every student and their learning style. The only type of ability that is really valued in our society is that high analytical ability that is generally correlated with math and languages. However, only a small fraction of students fit this category. The speaeker talks about bringing equity in the classroom. The speaker Dr. Lindsey F. Ott is a teacher so she understands this discussion on a personal level since she is affected by it.
